The perch (white bar) sits at the top and follows your palm. The puppet hangs from it on a long center string that sags and swings; two thinner control lines run to the arms, so lifting the perch lifts the limbs.

Tilt your hand and the control rotates with it: roll (in-plane lean) tips the "+" and leans the puppet through the strings; pitch (nod fwd/back) and yaw (turn left/right) foreshorten the bar and shift/turn the body. The dynamic puppet stays in the screen plane — only the control leaves it.

The hand overlay (bottom-right) shows all 21 landmarks; the control point (landmark #9, the palm) is ringed in green, and its crosshair maps to the perch crosshair on stage.

The question this spike answers: with the strings and overlay visible, can you act with intent — swing an arm where you want it, settle a swing — or is it chaos?
